Startup Spotlights: 54 Startups
Bubbles

Bubbles is a tech startup at the University of Illinois that aims to bring a more seamless, productive, and efficient user experience for virtual meetings. The concept of their app, also named Bubbles, is to allow users to attend multiple Zoom meetings at the same time, with different bubbles corresponding to different meetings on a well-organized dashboard.

According to Sumedh Vaidyanathan, a founding member of Bubbles, the original idea came just this year when attendees at the 2020 Virtual Engineering Career Fair at UIUC reported how inefficient and how impersonal the event felt for them. Now, with a developed prototype and an upcoming production release, the Bubbles team hopes to further expand and strengthen their startup by presenting their product at the upcoming Forge Conference!

Hang

Hang is a tech startup that reimagines how people from all walks of life have genuine social interactions. According to Carson Wang, one of the Founders of Hang, the startup is built around an app, with the same name, that allows users to meet people in-person with similar interests.

Hang also plans on participating in the College New Venture Challenge managed jointly by the University of Illinois and the University of Chicago.

Allergenius Baking Co.

Allergenius Baking Co. is a food startup that presents a new fascinating take on desserts: making them allergy-free. According to Josh James, a member of Founders and the co-founder of Allergenius Baking Co., the idea came about when two of his co-founders had severe food allergies despite being avid bakers.

Because there were few baking companies out in the market that baked goods tailored to consumers with allergies, Allergenius Baking Co. made it their mission to provide a variety of baked desserts that all people can enjoy by not including the 14 most common allergies in any of their recipes. Having won several pitch competitions since 54, Allergenius Baking Co. plans to continue their success and bring their products to larger markets by continuing to develop their startup at Forge and iVenture.
